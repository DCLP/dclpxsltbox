DCLP Transcription [xml]

Introduction

Medical fragment. Papyrus fragment (9.5 x 9.4 cm) preserving on the recto the first twelve lines of one column practically complete, and the beginnings of the first twelve of the next. The verso seems to contain accounts assignable to the 2nd or 3rd cent. AD. The middle point occurs at col. I, l. 7; a paragraphos after col. I, l. 7 and 11, marking a pause in or after the line it follows. The brevity of this fragment makes the positive determination of its meaning difficult; anyway, it seems to be part of a medical treatise. The hand is a semi-uncial and suggests a date in the 2nd cent. AD.
