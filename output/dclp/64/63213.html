Introduction

Medical treatise. Fragment (14.8 x 9.8) of a papyrus roll broken on all sides, containing on the recto two columns of writing (traces of ink on the verso). In col. II, paragraphoi are added between ll. 12-3, 15-6, 19-20; a diaeresis also occurs at l. 19. The lacunosity of this papyrus makes the positive determination of its contents extremely difficult, but it seems to be part of a medical treatise, which is now lost. The text was copied in a quite inelegant book-hand with some cursive traits assignable to the 1st or 2nd cent. AD.
