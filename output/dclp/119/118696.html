DCLP Transcription [xml]

Introduction

Medical recipes. The papyrus fragment (17.4 x 14 cm), written on both sides by the same hand, is probably part of a roll. It preserves 20 lines on the recto and 15 on the verso; the columns is ca. 11 cm wide and the intercolumnium measures ca. 3.5 cm. Upper, lower and right margins are lost. The manuscript contains a collection of recipes. The text on the front, partly overlapping with Aet. XII 67, is concerned with the treatment of the sore throat, arthritis, epilepsy and headache; the back preserves three remedies against cough and bronchial affections introduced by short titles and separated by horizontal strokes. Phonetic mistakes occur in both sides. The papyrus is written in a semi-literary script assigned to the 6th-7th c. AD.

(This papyrus has been digitally edited by Francesca Corazza as part of the Project "DIGMEDTEXT - Online Humanities Scholarship: A Digital Medical Library based on Ancient Texts" (ERC-AdG-2013, Grant Agreement no. 339828) funded by the European Research Council at the University of Parma (Principal Investigator: Prof. Isabella Andorlini). The digital edition is mostly based on the previous edition (F. Mitthof, GMP II 9))

Notes

  • 5-6.

    Cf. Aet.: ἀκριβεῖς καῖ εὐμαθεστέρους.

  • 6.

    The first letter is not clearly readable; it might be θ or τ, thus προ]θυμοταίρως or προ]τυμοταίρως (lege προθυμοτέρους) are possibly contained.

  • 6-7.

    καθαίρω δι' οὔρων: cf. Aet. II 240.44f.

  • 7-10.

    Cf. Aet. οὐδ' ἄλλο τι λυπεῖ μέρος.

  • 12.

    Cf. Aet.: καὶ τὸ ὅλον σῶμα ὑγιεινότερον.

  • 14-15.

    Cf. Aet. ἄλλους δὲ χρονίων καὶ πάνυ χαλεπῶν ἀλγημάτων τῆς κεφαλῆς ἐρρύσατο οὐκ ἐλπιζοντας ὑγιασθῆναι.

  • 20.

    The first component in Aetius is χαμαίδρυος (οὐγκ.) θ.
