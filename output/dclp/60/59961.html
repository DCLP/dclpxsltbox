Introduction

(P.Ant. 3.186) Galen, De compositione medicamentorum per genera 7.10. 37 fragments from a papyrus codex of large dimensions, whose surface is much discolored. Frr. 16-37, regarded as too small or illegible for identification by the first editor, were published by Manetti for the first time. According to the supplements, each line should contain 11-17 letters, and the upper, lower and lateral margins are respectively ca. 4.2, 3.5 and 2.9 cm wide, therefore each sheet should measure 33 x 21 cm approximately. P.Ant. III 139 fr. 1 is probably part of the same codex. Fr. 1 (3.5 x 5.1 cm) preserves the upper margin; fr. 2 (13.3 x 13.2 cm) contains 17 lines and the remains of upper and right margins; fr. 3 (4.5 x 4.7 cm) is broken on all sides an contains 8 lines; fr. 4 (7.4 x 9.6 cm) preserves the upper margin; fr. 5 (7 x 4.7 cm) contains the remains of the lateral margin; fr. 6 (3.5 x 2.8 cm) is damaged on all sides; fr. 7 (1.5 x 3 cm) partly preserves the upper margin; fr. 8 (3.7 x 4.2 cm) is broken on all sides; fr. 9 (4.7 x 9.4 cm) contain part of the upper margin and is totally illegible on side B written across the fibers; fr. 10 (11.5 x 16 cm) contains 22 lines and the lower margin; fr. 11 consists of two small pieces (4.2 x 3.8 cm + fr. 11bis 4.6 x 3.6 cm); frr. 12 (5 x 6.7 cm) and 13 (3.8 x 7.5 cm) belong to the same folium; fr. 14 (4.7 x 5 cm) contains part of the external margin; fr. 15 (8.6 x 6.7 cm) preserves the upper margin; fr. 16 (3.9 x 5.5 cm) contains 10 lines and is illegible on side A (across the fibers); fr. 17 (2.1 x 2.1 cm) preserves the remains of 3 lines; fr. 18 (2.5 x 3.2 cm) has traces of 6 lines; fr. 19 (1.9 x 2.8 cm) contains the remains of 5 lines and is joint to fr 11; fr. 20 (2.1 x 1.8 cm) is broken on all sides; fr. 21 (3.1 x 2.2 cm) is blank on the recto; fr. 22 (1.9 x 1.8 cm) is broken on all sides; fr. 23 (1.2 x 3.7 cm) contains traces of 7 lines; fr. 24 (1.9 x 1.7 cm) preserves the remains of 3 lines; fr. 25 (2 x 3 cm) is broken on all sides; frr. 26 (2.7 x 1.7 cm) and 30 are written by another hand; frr. 26, 27 (3.6 x 3.1 cm), 28 (2.2 x 2.1), 29 (1.7 x 1.6 cm), 33 (1.1 x 1.8 cm), 34 (2.4 x 0.8 cm), 36 (1.2 x 2.1 cm), 37 (2.3 x 2.5 cm) are broken on all sides; frr. 32 is blank on the recto and 35 (1.1 x 2.1 cm) is illegible on the verso. The diplè obelismene, along with filling marks and dicolon, marks the beginning of new sections. Phonetic mistakes, sometimes corrected by the same hand, and the abbreviation of final ν at the end of the line frequently occur. Two letters in the upper margin of fr. 12 A could indicate the number of the folium (ρ̣λ̣ = 130), while a marginal note might be contained in fr. 12 B. The Galenic text contained in the papyrus differs in a few points from the MSS version and. along with omissions and mistakes of little importance, it preserves a few interesting variants. The codex is written in a rather small, sloping book hand and can be assigned on paleographical grounds to the 6th c. AD.

(P.Ant. 3.139) Galen, De compositione medicamentorum per genera 7.10. The two fragments (fr. 1: 3 x 4; fr. 2: 4.8 x 5 cm) apparently belong to the same codex on papyrus. The left margin survives in frr. 1 recto and 2 verso; the right margin is preserved in frr. 1 verso and 2 recto; the upper and lower margins are lost. According to the first editor's transcription, the medical text on fr. 1 was identified by Morelli with Galen, De compositione medicamentorum per genera 7.10, while the content of fr. 2 is still unknown. Morelli suggests that P.Ant 139 fr. 1 and P.Ant. 186 fr. 10, which preserve portions of the same Galen's text, could originally belong to a single codex page. Regrettably, P.Ant. 139 is now lost, therefore a comparison between the two manuscripts is no longer possible. The papyrus is written in a medium-sized sloping book hand dated to the 6th c. AD.
