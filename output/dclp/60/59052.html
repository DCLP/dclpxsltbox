Introduction

Medical treatise. Fragment (35 x 24.5 cm) of a papyrus roll containing along the fibers remains of five consecutive columns of writing (on the verso, 4th cent. accounts from the Hermopolite Nome = SB 20 14299). On the recto, an unknown treatise on fevers, which discusses different types of πυρετοί (quotidian, tertian, quartan etc.). More notably, at col. IV, ll. 27-32 the author explicitly quoted Hippocrates (fort. Epid. 1.11 [2, 670.16-672.3 L]) to demonstrate the possible combination of tertian and quintan fevers; it is worth noticing that Galen openly rejected this interpretation of the Hippocratic passage in his commentary to Epidemics I (see Gal. In Hipp.Epid. I comment. CMG 5.10.1, 111-3, and Manetti 2008, 226-7). The editor princeps tentatively attributed the text to Agathinus Lacedaemonius (see K. Kalbfleisch, Papyri Argentoratenses graecae, Rostoch, 1901, 11-2). The script is a neat and stylish book-hand assignable to the 2nd cent. AD.
