Introduction

Magico-medical prescriptions. Fragmentary leaf (13.3 x 11.8) from a papyrus codex containing the upper part of two columns on each page. Both sides have an upper margin of ca. 3.3 cm; the recto preserves a right margin of 2.3 cm; the verso a left one of 1 cm; the intercolumnium is ca. 1 cm wide. The original order of the two sides is uncertain. The fragment preserves thirteen prescriptions separated by paragraphoi whose content is concerned with: a drying powder for the eyes, remedies against fever, demonic possession, tumors, strangury, migraine and wounds. Lectional signs (paragraphoi, dicola, stops, asterisk) are frequently employed in both sides. The papyrus is written in a sloping semi-literary hand dated to the 5th c. AD.

(This papyrus has been digitally edited by Francesca Corazza as part of the Project "DIGMEDTEXT - Online Humanities Scholarship: A Digital Medical Library based on Ancient Texts" (ERC-AdG-2013, Grant Agreement no. 339828) funded by the European Research Council at the University of Parma (Principal Investigator: Prof. Isabella Andorlini). The digital edition is mostly based on the previous editions (ed.pr. = J.W.B. Barns, P.Ant. II 66; ed.alt. = R.W. Daniel-F. Maltomini, Suppl. Mag. 2 94))
