Introduction

Medical treatise. 18 fragments (fr. 2b: 12 x 15 cm) from a papyrus codex. The papyrus preserves one of the upper and lower corners of the original sheets in frr. 1, 4 and 5, whereas frr. 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, and 10 come from the middle part including portions of the lateral margins, which are rather generous (about 2.5 cm). The medical content deals with diet in connection with fever and, according to Kudlien, the subject of the work may have been περὶ διαφορᾶς πυρετῶν. In spite of a few points of resemblance with Galen, Kudlien considers his authorship unlikely. The papyrus is written in a medium-sized, well-spaced hand of 'Coptic uncial' type assigned to the 6th c. AD.

(This papyrus has been digitally edited by Francesca Corazza as part of the Project "DIGMEDTEXT - Online Humanities Scholarship: A Digital Medical Library based on Ancient Texts" (ERC-AdG-2013, Grant Agreement no. 339828) funded by the European Research Council at the University of Parma (Principal Investigator: Prof. Isabella Andorlini). The digital edition is mostly based on the previous editions (ed.pr. = J.W.B. Barns, P. Ant. III 124; corr. = F. Kudlien ap. Barns; corr. = D. Manetti, Corpus dei papiri filosofici 1.2.1, 18.23T, p. 178-180))
